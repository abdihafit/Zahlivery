# Play Store Metadata Fix Plan

Google flagged the `en-GB` store listing screenshots because the listing does
not clearly and accurately represent what the app does. For Zahlivery, the
fastest way to resolve this is to replace the screenshots and tighten the
listing copy so every claim matches a visible in-app flow.

## What likely caused the rejection
- The screenshots may be too generic, promotional, or logo-heavy instead of
  showing the real product UI.
- The screenshots may mention features more strongly than the app currently
  supports.
- The listing text may overclaim features such as live rider tracking or
  in-app payments.
- The screenshots may not make it obvious that Zahlivery supports three roles:
  customer, shop or hotel, and rider.

## Safe screenshot sequence for `en-GB`
Use real screenshots from the app, not mocked marketing art. Prefer portrait
phone screenshots with short captions that describe only what is visible.

## Screenshot rules to follow
- Show the real app interface on every screenshot.
- Keep each caption short and factual.
- Use the same language as the store listing locale.
- Do not mention features that are not visible on that screenshot.
- Do not add "best", "fastest", "#1", or other unverifiable claims.
- Do not show empty placeholder screens if you can capture populated ones.
- Do not use admin-only screens unless they are part of the public app promise.

## Metadata text to avoid
Remove or avoid phrases like:
- Track your rider live
- Real-time GPS delivery map
- Instant in-app payments
- Order from anywhere
- All restaurants near you

Use safer phrasing instead:
- View order status updates
- Chat with shops and riders
- Assign available riders
- Contact the rider by phone or chat

## Resubmission checklist
- Step 1: Replace all rejected `en-GB` screenshots with real in-app UI.
- Step 2: Update short description and full description to match the current app build.
- Step 3: Confirm the title is `Zahlivery` everywhere and free of typos.
- Step 4: Make sure the icon matches the app brand and does not imitate another app.
- Step 5: Review every locale, not just `en-GB`, for the same mismatch.
- Step 6: Recheck the feature graphic, promo text, and description for overclaims.
- Step 7: Submit with a short reviewer note explaining that the screenshots and listing
  were updated to reflect the real customer, shop, and rider workflows.

## Play Console action order
- Upload the replacement screenshots first.
